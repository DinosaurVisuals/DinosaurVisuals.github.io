MoveME Project Description

Move ME is an educational project focused on the upper limb's anatomy, specifically the elbow (uniaxial joint), wrist (biaxial joint), and shoulder (triaxial joint), with an emphasis on understanding how joint actions are produced and constrained. It addresses the challenges of teaching musculoskeletal anatomy, which involve comprehending the intricate spatial relationships and dynamic interactions between skeletal and muscular systems. The project aims to develop an interactive module-based website featuring 2D/3D visualizations, self-exploration tools, and static illustrations to enhance spatial comprehension and reduce cognitive load. Designed for third-year kinesiology and medical students, Move ME provides a supportive resource to deepen understanding and facilitate the application of knowledge to complex problem-solving tasks. Additionally, instructors can integrate the visual tools into their teaching, promoting a more engaging and effective learning experience.
